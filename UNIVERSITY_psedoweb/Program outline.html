Program Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program provides students with a strong foundation in the principles and practices of computing. Students will gain expertise in software development, algorithms, data structures, and computer systems. The program prepares graduates for a wide range of careers in the technology industry and for advanced studies in computer science.

This program emphasizes hands-on learning, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills. Students will work on individual and group projects, and have the opportunity to participate in research and internships.

Core Curriculum

  • Introduction to Programming
  • Data Structures and Algorithms
  • Computer Organization and Architecture
  • Database Management Systems
  • Software Engineering
  • Operating Systems
  • Computer Networks
  • Theory of Computation

Electives

Students choose electives to specialize in areas of interest.

  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Machine Learning
  • Web Development
  • Mobile Application Development
  • Cybersecurity
  • Data Science
  • Computer Graphics
  • Human-Computer Interaction

Program Outcomes

  • Design, implement, and test software systems of varying complexity.
  • Apply algorithmic principles and data structures to solve computational problems.
  • Understand the organization and architecture of computer systems.
  • Manage and utilize databases effectively.
  • Apply software engineering principles to develop high-quality software.
  • Understand the principles of operating systems and computer networks.
  • Analyze the theoretical limits of computation.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the program are prepared for a variety of careers, including:

  • Software Engineer
  • Web Developer
  • Database Administrator
  • Systems Analyst
  • Network Engineer
  • Data Scientist
  • AI/Machine Learning Specialist
  • Cybersecurity Analyst
